The Problem

A typical school counsellor works with between 35 to 70 students simultaneously. Everyone receives the same vague personal statement edits, the same university suggestions based only on grades, and the same generic scholarship lists. This one-size-fits-all approach fails the students who need the most precise, personalised guidance to reach their dream university.

The Studera Solution

Studera connects high school students with current undergraduate students from their exact target university and programme — mentors who share the same country of origin, schooling system, and background. They know exactly what the admissions team looks for, and they can give you the resources no generic counsellor even knows about.

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Studera is a mentorship platform that connects high school students with current undergraduate students from their exact dream university and programme. Unlike generic college counselling, every mentor on Studera shares the same university, programme, country of origin, and schooling background as the student — making the guidance uniquely precise and effective.

Studera is designed for two groups. Mentees are high school students in Grade 9 to 12 who are preparing for university applications and want targeted, personalised guidance. Mentors are current undergraduate students in Year 1 to 4 who are enrolled at a target university and want to share their experience while earning.

Full pricing details will be announced at launch. What we can say is that Studera is designed to be accessible — our mission is to democratise high-quality college guidance, not restrict it to those who can afford premium services. Stay tuned for more details.

Studera V1 is targeted for launch on August 5, 2026. We are currently in our R&D phase, with alpha testing beginning May 20 and beta testing starting June 10. Sign up for our newsletter below to be the very first to know when we go live.

We have a strict and meritocratic mentor selection process. Every mentor application is evaluated on four criteria: their university GPA, their high school final grades, whether they are on a scholarship, and a Statement of Interest in which they describe the unique opportunities and insights they bring to high school students. Only mentors who meet our quality bar are approved — because your dream university deserves nothing less.
